Recipe Radar

Recipe Radar is an app designed and prototyped in Figma to help college students find easy recipes that fit their dietary needs and schedule time to cook the recipes in a customizable meal prep timeline.

The project won third place overall out of nine teams in Design@UCI’s quarterly project competition in Spring 2023.

As the Project Manager, I ensured that our team met consistently, completed tasks in a timely manner, and followed cohesive interface designs and intuitive user flows.

PROBLEM

College students have a hard time, finding time in their busy schedules to meal prep and cook healthy meals at home.

SOLUTION

Create a fun and accessible application that caters to the users dietary needs. Provide meal prepping recipes that can be stored for later use.

PROCESS

  • Week 1: Brainstorm app ideas and identify the audience.
  • Week 2: Ideate what resources we want for our app.
  • Week 3: Conduct competitive analysis research on current meal prepping apps.
  • Week 4: Distribute meal prep survey. Mimic the experience of looking up recipes.
  • Week 5: Brainstorm functions and corresponding functions.
  • Week 6: Working on mid fidelity wireframes.
  • Week 7: Working on high fidelity wireframes & prototyping.
  • Week 8: Conduct usability testing & revise accordingly.

Brainstorming & Ideation

  • Considered problems that college students face in regard to healthy eating.
  • Brainstormed how meal prepping works.
  • How college students typically meal prep.
  • How would we want meal prepping to be easier for ourselves.

Research

  1. SURVEY
    • Purpose: Understand college student perspective & priorities to determine necessary features
    • Findings
      • 40% of respondents believe they do not have good time management skills.
      • 50% of respondents try to prep 2-4 meals per week
      • 36% of respondents want more time t0 meal prep
      • 30% of respondents believe they spend too much money on dining out
      • 25% of respondents have completely different allergies and food restrictions from each other.
    • Conclusion
      • We need to develop an intuitive design to encourage users to meal plan at their own pace and schedule.
      • We need a questionnaire to capture each user's food preferences and dietary restrictions because there is an infinite amount of possibilities of what users can and cannot eat.
  2. COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS
    • Purpose: Compare meal prepping apps on the market & note similar features between apps
    • Competitors' Key Features
      • Personalization: Competitor A considers users' food preferences & preferred units of measure​
      • Available Resources: Competitor B considers users' kitchen appliances & cooking utensils​
      • Scheduling: Competitor C groups recipes for users to prioritize meal planning​
    • Conclusion
      • We need our application to incorporate all of the mentioned key features to widen our target audience and their needs.
  3. BECOME THE USER
    • Purpose: Mimic user’s perspective when searching for recipes
    • Process
      • Noted information hierarchy
      • What features we liked and disliked
      • Considered how we could enhance this experience through our product
    • Conclusion
      • We need to ensure straightforward information organization and visualization in the application design.
